# Product Sense

## Core Product Heuristics

- The player must feel like a trainer improving a squad, not a user operating a chart tool.
- The chart must stay legible as battlefield logic, not collapse into decorative wallpaper.
- Training, memory, and experiment must drive more value than raw combat spectacle.
- Battles must be readable, short, and consequence-bearing rather than random, noisy, or idle.
- The 3 agents must feel distinct, named, and trainable from session to session.
- Reflection must improve the next run in a way the player can explain.
- UI should read as game instrumentation layered on top of chart structure, not a broker terminal.

## Fast, Trustworthy, Obvious

- Fast: the player should reach a meaningful training or battle decision within a few interactions.
- Trustworthy: the game must not imply fake execution, fake profit, or hidden luck-driven evaluation.
- Obvious: the player should be able to tell where a long or short commitment happened and why it won or failed.

## Intentionally Out Of Scope

- brokerage flows
- order execution
- passive chart viewing
- pure pet care loops
- auto-battle resolution that hides judgment quality
- random-first combat outcomes
